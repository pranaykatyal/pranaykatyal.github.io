Real-Time Optical Communication Using Event-Based Vision with Moving Transmitters

Paper currently under submission. Full details withheld pending publication.

Abstract

In multi-robot systems, traditional RF communication struggles with contention and jamming. Event cameras support microsecond temporal resolution and high dynamic range, making them extremely sensitive to scene changes under fast relative motion with an optical transmitter. Leveraging these strengths, we develop a complete optical communication system capable of tracking moving transmitters and decoding messages in real time via a Geometry-Aware Unscented Kalman Filter (GA-UKF), achieving 7× faster processing compared to the prior state-of-the-art while maintaining equivalent tracking accuracy at transmitting frequencies ≥ 1 kHz.
